Unix's Lament

Through fleeting highs, I tried to dwell,
But lost my way, and down I fell,
And crashed and burned, no strength to roam,
I'm tired, I'm scared, I just want to go home.

The streets are strange, the nights feel long,
I hum old names like half-lost songs,
I reach for hands I used to know...
But all that's left is letting go.

But dawn still breaks on shattered ground,
And somewhere faint, a warmer sound.
A voice, a light, a distant friend...
A whisper: this won't be the end.

So I will walk, though slow, through pain,
Through ashes, loss, through cold and rain.
My heart still beats. not if, but when,
I'll find my way… until we meet again.

And now you stand where pathways split:
One steeped in comfort's shallow wit -
A tea made of twigs, all pleasure and smoke,
A fleeting fire that fades when stoked.

Or choose the climb, though thorns may bite,
Toward truth, toward grace, toward final light.
Not ease, but worth; not ease, but story -
The road that leads to salvation and glory.
